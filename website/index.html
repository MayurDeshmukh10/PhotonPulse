Story of our character


Ephemeral Dusk: A Voyage through the Shadows of Old Edo

in the dying embers of twilight, an anime protagonist meanders through the ancient thoroughfares of Old Edo, adorned in a garb that seamlessly melds the epochs. The scene unfolds a prophecy woven into the very fabric of the streets, where lanterns cast ephemeral glows on the bricked paths, revealing the mysteries of a bygone Japan. Wooden houses stand as stoic sentinels, cherry blossoms frozen in perpetual bloom. As the sun descends, the character's trajectory morphs into a celestial pathway, beckoning exploration through the veiled corridors of time and space. This captivating journey into the unknown captures the essence of exploration, where reality and fantasy coalesce in a dance of shadows and whispers.

Features


Thinlens camera

Thinlens camera adds a realistic touch to your rendering capabilities by simulating how real-world cameras capture images. By mimicking the principles of optics, this feature enables depth of field effects and enhances the overall realism of your rendered scenes

Rough Dielectric

Rough dielectrics are another type of BSDF that can simulate very interesting materials such as frosted glass or add even more realism to other types of dielectrics by adding a bit of roughness to the surface.

Halton Sampler

The Halton Sampler allows to generate such low-discrepancy sample points for an arbitrary number of integral dimensions.

Shading Normals

shading normal (a.k.a normal mapping) to you renderer can greatly enhances its realism as it can add a lot of detail without requiring complex meshes. The main idea is that instead of directly using the normals of the mesh you load an additional texture that describes the normals of the underlying geometry instead

Image Denoising

Denoising can greatly improve the quality of low sample count renders. Arbitrary output variables (AOVs) such as albedo and normals are feed to the denoiser improve the quality of the denoised images and avoid overly blurry denoised images.








Post Processing - Bloom Effect

used to reproduce an imaging artifact of real-world cameras.
